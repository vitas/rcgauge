Make your own Bluetooth RC Throwmeter

by Yannick Selles, Pierre Rondel, Vitaliy Ryumshyn and Alois Hahn

 

Introduction

This mobile application allows you to "build" your own Bluetooth throwmeter for a very reasonable cost. It doesn't require any soldering or cabling. You just need to have access to a 3D printer and that is all!

The Sensor Component

The component is the BWT61CL from WitMotion, a six axis Bluetooth attitude angle sensor with battery incorporated. You can buy it on AliExpress or Amazon US and amazon EU and cost around 20~25 Euros, free shipping.

 

Starting with version 1.6 the app can use WitMotion BWT901CL also. Please note, when using two sensors at the same time (dual-mode), the sensor models must be the same, either both BWT61CL or both BWT901CL

 

It is based on the JY61 sensor, has Bluetooth or serial connectivity, integrates a dynamic Kalman filter algorithm, an internal voltage stabilizing circuit module, voltage 3.3v~5v. The only drawback is, that the Bluetooth BLE is only compatible with android. The battery has a 150mA capacity so it lasts for a long operation time.

 

For more datasheet information refer to the manufacturer website or see github repo.

Building your RC Throwmeter

The work is limited to the 3D printing of the clips that allow to fix the device on the trailing edge of the glider. Download STL files and see more ideas for using the RC Throwmeter on the github repo.

 

 

 

 

The clips are removable, so the device, its clips and the charging cable can be carried/stored in the plastic box it was received in.

 

Just print the part, in PLA or PETG or ABS, 100% infill for me. The dimension is optimized for F3x plane wings and tailplanes. To protect the surface of the control surface and avoid the clips to slide/shift, I added a piece of rubber (a small piece of bicycle inner tube) on the surfaces of the clip, with double side tape.

 

Print the sticker using the .pdf provided. Prior to apply it, don't forget to remove the other sticker. Protect the printed paper with transparent tape at the top, and double side tape on the other side, and position it on the top of the case, respecting the correct orientation (Charge, On/Off). This will give you the orientation when using pointing where is the hinge

 

The App

The Android App has been developed by Yannick and Vitaliy. The App is divided in several screens, the start screen giving access to the top left menu, the "BT Sensors" screen to connect the App with the desired throwmeter(s), Preferences provide user settings, and finally the "Throw meter" screen where everything happens once sensors have been paired in android bluetooth menu.

 

 

Using your RC Throwmeter(s)

Charging the battery 

Connect the cable provided to the balancer Plug. On newer versions just connect the USB cable. The red LED is ON during the charge and switches to OFF once charged.

 

Switching on your Throwmeter(s)

Move the sliding switch from right to left. The blue LED is blinking which indicates that the device is waiting for the binding. Do not forget to switch off after work has been done, otherwise batteries will be discharged.

 

Binding your Android smartphone for the first time

On your smartphone navigate to the parameter/bluetooth menu and scan for new devices. The BWT61CL and BWT901CL will show as HC-06. When asked, enter the code 1234. The sensor is now paired and you are ready to open the App.

 

Using App the first time

Starting with Android version 12, the user must allow Nearby Devices permission. Please allow Nearby Devices. Otherwise, the app cannot access the WitMotion sensors and will refuse work and a pop-up window with warnings appears.

If you have denied once, Android will not ask again. If it happens, please un-install the app and re-install it or change the permission in the Android Apps menu. 

 

 

 

Binding with the App 

Select the menu on the top left, then open "BT Sensors" and select one (or two) HC-06 sensors. Once selected you can return on the start page. Open the menu on the top left and select "Throw meter"

 

 

 

 

 

Do Measurements

 

Select the menu on the top left, then open "Throw meter". The app starts binding the throwmeters. It is indicated by a blinking red colored status line on top. Wait until the app completes the binding, indicated by the status line changing blue, not blinking any more. It needs about 10 seconds. If it fails, return to main menu and try again.

Now the app displays the measured angles (Angle) and calculates rudder deflection (Travel), depending on the chord length (Ruder Chord), entered by the user.

 

 

 

 

In dual sensor mode the app can display differences between two sensors. Press Diff button (bottom right) to toggle between normal and difference displays. Angle difference between left and right sensors is shown between the Angle display fields. Travel difference between left and right sensor is shown between the Travel fields.

 

 

If Diff is pressed again, the difference display mode changes to a percentage display instead showing absolute values. For calculation it compares the absolute of the throw values. Then it uses the larger value from either side, left or right, as reference and calculates the percentage. If both sides are equal, it will show 0%. The < indicates that the reference value (larger value) is on the left, > indicates the reference is on the right.

 

 

 

Examples:

 

left aileron = +40mm, right aileron = -40mm, diff = 0%

left aileron = +40mm, right aileron = -20mm, diff = 50%

left aileron = +40mm, right aileron = 0mm, diff = 100%

 

left aileron = -40mm, right aileron = +40mm, diff = 0%

left aileron = -20mm, right aileron = +40mm, diff = 50%

left aileron = 0mm, right aileron = +40mm, diff = 100%

 

left aileron = +40mm, left flap = +8mm, diff = 80%

left aileron = +40mm, left flap = +4mm, diff = 90%

left aileron = +40mm, left flap = 0mm, diff = 100%

 

 

 

Calibration

 

If the device is providing inconsistent or weird measures, it probably needs a full calibration. Level the sensor(s) horizontally and do not move. To activate Calibration long press the Reset/Calib button, confirm dialog and wait until calibration completes.

 

 

 

 

Reset Angle

The device is supposed to do a calibration at startup. It is recommended to do it with the device installed on a horizontal surface. Once done, you can clip the device on the leading edge of control surface you want to measure. When your control surface is at its neutral position, proceed to a new "Reset" in order for the device to know its spatial position and be ready to measure the angle.

 

 

 

Travel setup 

Measure the chord of you control surface and click on the chord field at the top of the screen to enter the value. There is no unit, so it can be mm or inches. Travel will be shown in the same unit.

 

 

Max UP and Max DOWN

This is a very useful feature that allows you to quickly measure the maximum up and down travels of a control surface. Use Reset to clear the Max UP/DOWN values for a new measurement.

 

 

Set Max UP and Max DOWN Thresholds

If your objective is to do settings, not to measure, you can enter a Max positiv travel and a Min negativ travel separately. This instructs the App to display an alarm (in RED color instead GREEN) when the travel value exceeds a threshold, either above the Max positiv travel or below the Min negativ travel.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Sensor Settings

This screen provides user options.

Axes

The App computes its spatial position upon the rotation measurements of the WitMotion sensor. The most precise method uses all three axes X, Y and Z. It turned out, that the WitMotion sensor can lose the correct Z value when moved fast and irregularly. This may add significant error to the measured angle, specifically around zero. The user has to use Reset if this occurs.

·        Use XY axes (default)

The App ignores the vertical Z axis. This method is much more robust than XYZ, but might add some absolute measurement error. In real life context this can be accepted for the comfort of robustness, thus it is the default setting.

·        Use XYZ axes

The most precise method uses all three axes X, Y and Z.

 

Throw Calculation Method

The throw calculation of the deflection may use one of these formulas.  

·        Orthogonal deflection (default)
      (A) throw = chord * sin(alpha)

·        Chord length
       (B) throw = chord * 2 * sin(alpha/2)

WitMotion Sensor Model

The App can detect the WitMotion sensor model automatically. If desired, the user may choose a specific model.

·        Auto-Detect (default)
      The app recognizes the proper WitMotion model automatically.

·        BWT61CL
      The app assumes usage of BWT61CL.

·        BWT901CL
      The app assumes usage of BWT901CL.

Important Note:
In Dual-Sensor Mode both sensors must be of same type, both either BWT61CL or BWT901CL.

 

First Time Sensor Configuration

 

Usually, the WitMotion sensors can be used out of the box, with factory settings. If in doubt or in case of strange readings, the sensor can be configured with this app.

When connected, long press the blue status indicator on top to activate a configuration dialog. Check if the sensor type, BWT61CL or BWT901CL, is correct and confirm with OK.

Note that some (newer) BWT61CL identify as BWT901CL. If that happens, please keep BWT901CL. It does not matter, as they respond to both protocols correctly.

 

 

Leave App and Switch Power-Off the WitMotion Sensors

 

IMPORTANT:

At the end of your work, switch off the WitMotion sensors. Otherwise, the battery will be discharged. Unfortunately, the WithMotion sensors do not switch off automatically.

The app issues a warning box on exit to remind you.

 

 

 

FAQ  Frequently Asked Questions

 

Q: The app fails to connect devices at the Throw meter screen

A: Please leave screen and go back to main screen. Retry Throw meter again. In most cases, it will connect. The reason is unknown, possible radio interference happens. You can try to change the distance between your mobile phone and the sensors, e.g. 30100 cm. For unknown reasons connection fails happen in dual sensor configuration mainly. You could try to leave the app, disable/enable bluetooth on android phone, power-off/power-on sensors and re-try.

 

Q: On first time usage out of the box, readings are strange and do not follow sensor position correctly.

A: The sensor might be mis-configured, e.g. for vertical mounting instead horizontal. Send configuration string to the sensor as described in chapter First Time Sensor Configuration.

 

Q: The measured angles are strange or needs long time to stabilize.

A: This might be caused by bad calibration. Please repeat calibration of the sensor on a horizontal table.

Q: The update of the angles is not regular timed and stops occasionally or bluetooth disconnects happen.

A: This could be caused by radio interference with the RC transmitter. Try to move the RC transmitter apart from the sensors and the mobile phone.

Q: Angle readings are changing all the time and do not stabilize.

A: Consider shaking or vibrating servos. The sensor is not capable to resolve fast movements correctly and might be disturbed by vibrations. As this is WitMotion specific, please try to stop vibrations, e.g. replace servo. During min/max measurements try to move surfaces slow and smooth.
